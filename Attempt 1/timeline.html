The Internet

By Abdullah Ibna Aamir

Q&A

What is the Internet?
A global network of interconnected computers using protocols to communicate.
Source
Where was the Internet born?
In the US, through the Department of Defense’s ARPA.
Source
When was the Internet created?
Late 1980s, evolving from ARPANET.
Source
What is the World Wide Web (WWW)?
A system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the Internet.
Source
Protocol used on the WWW?
HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol) enables data transfer between client and server.
Source
What can students access on the Web?
Educational resources, research papers, articles, online libraries, databases, social media, news, and more.
Source
What is a URL?
A Uniform Resource Locator is a web address identifying a resource on the internet. Components: Protocol, Subdomain, Domain, Port, Path, File.
Source
Software to view webpages? Examples?
A web browser (e.g., Firefox, Chrome, Edge, Safari) is needed.
Source
What is an Internet domain?
A unique name for a group of computers or servers, used to identify an organisation, website or network.
Source
Describe 4 Internet domains.
  • .com – Commercial
  • .org – Non-profit organisation
  • .edu – Education
  • .gov – Government
Source
Social and ethical issues of the Internet?
Online harassment, privacy, misinformation, digital divide, cybercrime.
Source

Timeline: History of the Internet

1969: ARPANET (precursor to Internet) created.
1972: First public ARPANET demo, email introduced.
1983: DNS and domain names (.com, .org, etc.) created.
Late 1980s: "Internet" term spreads globally.
1990: Tim Berners-Lee creates the WWW.
1993: First graphical web browser (Mosaic).
2000s–Now: Internet expansion, social media, smartphones.
